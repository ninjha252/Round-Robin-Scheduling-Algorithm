Round Robin Scheduling Algorithm

The Round Robin Scheduling algorithm is a preemptive scheduling algorithm that is widely used for time-sharing systems. This algorithm assigns a fixed time unit per process and cycles through them. Every process is assigned a time slot or quantum and is interrupted if it is not completed before the quantum expires, after which it is added to the end of the queue.

It is designed to ensure that all processes are allocated a fair amount of CPU time in a cyclic order. This prevents the monopolization of the CPU by a single process and provides a predictable time frame for each process execution.
